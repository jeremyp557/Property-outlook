Core Contradiction

The reform is a demand-side intervention dressed as a supply-side solution. It redirects investor capital away from established stock — but supply is constrained by construction economics, labour costs, and planning, none of which the budget substantively addresses. Renters bear the cost of the transition period with no near-term relief.
